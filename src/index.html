A hamburger is a type of sandwich that consists of a fried chopped cutlet served inside a cut round loaf. It is usually accompanied by a variety of condiments such as lettuce, ketchup and mayonnaise, slices of pickled cucumber, tomato or zucchini, raw or fried onions.

The most common "quick" food in the United States, the hamburger is far from American in origin. Scientists attribute its authorship to the Scythian nomads, who placed fried meat between two slices of bread. And the "king" of fast food arrived in the New World together with German immigrants: it was named, by the way, in honor of Hamburg, the second largest city in Germany.
